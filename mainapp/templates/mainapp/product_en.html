WHAT IS BIOGUMUS AND WHAT IS ITS COMPOSITION?

{% endblock header_content %} {% block content %}

Biogumus is a product of processing organic masses by California red earthworms. It is a loose, finely granulated mass with granule sizes of 1-3 mm, possibly up to 5mm. Concentrated fertilizer, contains in a balanced combination a whole complex of nutrients - micro and macro elements, mineral fertilizers, amino acids, humic acid, humates and fulvates of sodium and potassium, natural phytohormones, spores of soil microorganisms useful for plants, gibberellins in an easily digestible form.
Thanks to intensive fermentation , it is enhanced:

  1. Plant growth and development
  2. Increases germination and seed germination energy
  3. Stimulates root formation in plants.
  4. Increases the immunity of plants.
  5. Increases fertility, looseness of the soil
  6. Keeps moisture in the soil for a long time.
  7. Reduces the content of nitrites in agricultural products.
  8. Prevents the entry of heavy metals and radionuclides into the plant.
  9. Increases the content of sugars, proteins and vitamins in fruits and vegetables.
  10. Eliminates chlorosis, stimulates flowering and fruiting.
  11. It gives an effect to the rapid ripening of fruits for 7-10 days
  12. Stress is relieved, especially when planting plants, seeds.
  13. The products grown on Biogumus practically increase the duration of storage.
  14. Biogumus is 10 times higher than manure in terms of nutrient content. Biogumus is 10 times more effective than manure. Moreover, fresh manure is a hotbed of fungal and viral diseases of plants. The Biogumus completely lacks viable weed seeds, has anti-septic properties, destroying viral and fungal diseases of plants.
Chemical composition of Biogumus
Humidity 40-45%
Ash content 35-45%
Organic substances 55-65%
Humic substances 25-32%
Common nitrogen 1.0-2.0%
Total phosphorus (P2O5) 1.5-3.0%
Total potassium (K2O) 1.2-2.0%
Calcium 4.0-6.0%
Magnesium 0.6-2.3%
Iron 0.6-2.5%
Manganese 60-80 мг/кг
Mass fraction of heavy metals, mg/kg below the maximum permissible concentration for soils
Pathogenic microflora absent
Helminth eggs absent
